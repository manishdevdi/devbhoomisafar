Auli

About Auli

Auli, a beautiful hill station and ski resort in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, is famous for its pristine snow slopes and panoramic views of the Himalayas. Situated at an altitude of 2,800 meters, it is often referred to as the 'Skiing Capital of India'.

History & Significance

Auli was once a training ground for the Indo-Tibetan Border Police and still retains its military significance. Over the years, it has developed into a popular winter destination, attracting skiers from across the globe.

Activities & Attractions

  • Skiing and snowboarding (Dec–Feb)
  • Asia’s longest cable car ride – Joshimath to Auli
  • Artificial Lake – one of the world’s highest man-made lakes
  • Gurson Bugyal trek (3 km scenic hike)
  • Viewpoints for Nanda Devi, Mana Parvat, and Hathi Ghoda Peaks

What to Expect

  • Breathtaking views of snow-covered Himalayan peaks
  • Adventure-packed snow activities and chairlift rides
  • Peaceful surroundings and clean mountain air

Food and Culture

  • Try Garhwali favorites like Chainsoo, Kandalee Saag, and Gahat ki Dal
  • Local cafés serve simple yet hearty meals in traditional pahadi style
  • Interact with locals and experience their modest and warm lifestyle

How to Reach

  • Nearest Town: Joshimath (connected by road)
  • Ropeway Distance: ~4.5 km from Joshimath
  • Nearest Railway Station: Rishikesh
  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant, Dehradun
