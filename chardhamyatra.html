About Char Dham Yatra

The Char Dham Yatra is a sacred Hindu pilgrimage journey to four spiritual sites in Uttarakhand: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. Located in the Garhwal Himalayas, the Char Dham represents the source of four rivers and is believed to cleanse one's soul of all sins.

Spiritual Significance

These holy shrines are deeply rooted in Hindu mythology. Yamunotri is the source of River Yamuna, Gangotri of River Ganga, Kedarnath is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, and Badrinath is the abode of Lord Vishnu. Undertaking this yatra is believed to lead one toward moksha (salvation).

Yatra Route & Accessibility

  • Yamunotri: Accessible from Hanuman Chatti with a 6 km trek
  • Gangotri: Motorable road up to the shrine
  • Kedarnath: 16 km trek from Gaurikund or helicopter service available
  • Badrinath: Well connected by road up to the temple gate

What to Expect

  • Mesmerizing views of the Garhwal Himalayas and river valleys
  • Deep spiritual energy at every shrine
  • Opportunities for prayers, pooja, meditation, and holy dips

Food and Culture

  • Simple, sattvic (vegetarian) meals served by temple trusts and local dhabas
  • Garhwali culture with devotional bhajans, arti rituals, and ancient traditions
  • Local souvenirs like rudraksha, woolens, and organic Himalayan herbs

Best Time for Yatra

  • May to early November (when shrines are open)
  • Avoid monsoon (July–August) due to landslides
