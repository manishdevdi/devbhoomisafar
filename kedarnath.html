Explore Kedarnath Temple

About Kedarnath

Kedarnath Temple is one of the holiest Hindu shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand at an altitude of 3,583 meters. Nestled amidst the snow-capped Garhwal Himalayas near the Mandakini River, Kedarnath is a key pilgrimage site of the Char Dham and Panch Kedar circuits.

Spiritual & Historical Significance

The temple is believed to be built by the Pandavas and later revived by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century. According to legend, the Pandavas sought Lord Shiva’s forgiveness for their sins in the Mahabharata war, and he appeared here in the form of a bull before disappearing into the ground, leaving his hump behind.

Trek Details

  • Starting Point: Gaurikund
  • Length: 16-18 km (approx)
  • Difficulty: Moderate to Difficult

What to Expect

  • Breathtaking views of the Kedarnath Peak and surrounding ranges
  • Atmosphere charged with spiritual energy and devotion
  • Scenic trail through forests, waterfalls, and rugged Himalayan paths

Food and Culture

  • Experience simple sattvic meals provided at dharamshalas and camps
  • Interact with sadhus and pilgrims from all over India
  • Observe traditional Himalayan rituals and puja practices at the temple
  • Enjoy local dishes like Rajma-Chawal, Khichdi, and Mandua roti in nearby villages

Local Attractions Nearby

  • Vasuki Tal – A glacier lake trek (8 km from Kedarnath)
  • Bhairavnath Temple – Overlooking Kedarnath valley
  • Triyuginarayan Temple – Where Lord Shiva and Parvati were believed to be married
