Explore Tungnath Temple

About Tungnath

Tungnath, the highest Shiva temple in the world, is perched at an altitude of 3,680 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand. It is one of the Panch Kedar temples and believed to be over 1,000 years old. According to mythology, the temple was built by the Pandavas to appease Lord Shiva after the Kurukshetra war.

Spiritual & Historical Significance

The temple holds immense religious importance and is associated with legends from the Mahabharata. It is said that the arm of Lord Shiva appeared here when he disguised himself as a bull to avoid the Pandavas.

Trek Details

  • Starting Point: Chopta
  • Length: 3.5 km (approx)
  • Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
  • Best Time to Visit: April to November

What to Expect

  • Enchanting views of snow-capped peaks including Chaukhamba and Nanda Devi
  • Peaceful trek through lush meadows and rhododendron forests
  • A chance to meditate at one of the most serene spiritual spots in India

Food and Culture

  • Enjoy local Garhwali dishes like Mandua ki Roti, Aloo ke Gutke, and Jhangora Kheer
  • Experience the simple and heartfelt hospitality of Himalayan villagers
  • Witness traditional cooking methods using firewood and stone stoves in nearby homestays
  • Get introduced to Garhwali customs, attire, and folklore during village interactions

Local Attractions Nearby

  • Chandrashila Peak (1.5 km above Tungnath)
  • Deoria Tal
  • Rudraprayag & Ukhimath
